### Covariates of Academic Burnout Among University of California San Diego Students
Mar 2024 - May 2024

A pilot study recruiting 55 UCSD students examined potential correlates of academic burnout near finals week. Self-report data was analyzed using SciPy and Jamovi. Measures included the Copenhagen Burnout Inventory (CBI) and the Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI). Poor sleep quality was associated with greater levels of academic burnout (r = .43, p = .001). Commute times, number of enrolled units, and class standing were not significantly correlated with academic burnout. The sample reported burnout levels similar to that of child welfare workers and 73% reported moderate to high levels of burnout, highlighting the importance of interventions to support student wellbeing. Findings were presented at the California State University San Marcos 30th Annual Psychology Student Research Conference.

October 15, 2024 Update: I have improved the analysis by using multiple linear regression modeling. Additionally, in the original study, there were 6 extreme outliers in reported nightly sleep duration. In the original study, the outliers were interpreted as weekly sleep durations and transformed. With this updated analysis, their sleep scores were completely omitted. A significant association remains between poor sleep quality and academic burnout when controlling for commute times, enrolled unit count, and class standing.

# Cultural Background Matters When Sharing Culturally Relevant Subject Matter
Apr 2023 - May 2023

A 2 × 2 independent-group factorial design that recruited 90 participants tested the interaction between a presenter's perceived cultural identity and an audience member's racial identity on perceived presenter credibility. Data was analyzed using IBM SPSS Statistics. Analysis of main effects show that a presenter whose perceived cultural background reflects the content of a presentation will be perceived as more credible (p = .02). No interaction effect was found. Findings were presented at the Spring 2023 Palomar College Research Methods in Psychology Research Fair.


# Social Media Use, Attention, and Sleep Quality: Social Media Use Linked With Worse Sleep
Feb 2023 - Mar 2023

A correlational study that recruited 78 participants examined the associations between social media use, attention, and sleep quality. Measures included the Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI), the Mindful Attention Awareness Scale (MAAS), and automatically recorded smartphone app usage across four social media platforms. Data was analyzed using IBM SPSS Statistics. Higher durations of social media use were correlated with worse sleep outcomes (r = .25, p = .03). Findings were presented at the California State University, San Marcos 29th Annual Psychology Student Research Conference.
